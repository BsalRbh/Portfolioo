My path into software development was not a straight line. Before becoming a developer, I worked in teaching, accounting, and even banking, and each of those experiences taught me something valuable about discipline, communication, and problem-solving.

## Where I started

I began my career in roles that were far from software engineering. I worked as a junior accountant, a teacher, and an intern in banking. At the time, I did not know that I would eventually move into technology, but those jobs helped me build patience, responsibility, and attention to detail.

Teaching especially helped me learn how to explain ideas clearly. That skill turned out to be surprisingly useful later when I started writing code, debugging problems, and collaborating with others.

## How I discovered development

Over time, I became more interested in technology and how software is built. I started learning programming and exploring web development, and the more I learned, the more I wanted to build things myself.

I was drawn to frontend development first because I liked seeing immediate results on the screen. React and Next.js became important parts of my stack, and they helped me understand how modern web applications are structured and delivered.

## Moving into backend work

As I grew more confident, I started working on backend systems too. Learning Go gave me a deeper understanding of APIs, authentication, and server-side logic.

This shift made me feel like a real full-stack developer. Instead of only focusing on what users see, I began thinking about how the entire application works behind the scenes.

## What I learned along the way

One of the biggest lessons from my journey is that growth does not always happen in a straight line. Sometimes the experiences that seem unrelated at first become the ones that shape your strengths the most.

I also learned that becoming a developer is not only about writing code. It is about solving problems, learning continuously, and staying patient when things do not work on the first try.

## Where I am now

Today, I see myself as a full-stack developer who enjoys building useful web applications with React, Next.js, and Go. I still carry lessons from my earlier jobs, and they continue to influence how I work.

My journey is still going, but I am proud of how far I have come. Every project I build now feels like another step forward.

## Closing thoughts

If there is one thing I would tell someone starting their own path, it is this: your background does not have to fit a perfect pattern to lead you into tech. What matters is being willing to learn, adapt, and keep moving forward.
